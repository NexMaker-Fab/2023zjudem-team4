A Voltmeter is a voltage measuring device that is used to measure the voltage on certain points in an electrical circuit. Voltage is the potential difference that is created between two points in an electrical circuit. There are two types of voltmeters. Some voltmeters are designed to measure the voltage of DC circuits and other voltmeters are intended to measure the voltage in AC circuits. These voltmeters are further characterized into two categories. One is digital voltmeter which shows the measurements on a digital screen and the other is an analog voltmeter that uses a needle to point on the scale to show us the exact reading.

 

In this project, we are going to make a voltmeter using Arduino Uno. We will explain two configurations of a digital voltmeter in this article. In the first configuration, the microcontroller will be able to measure voltage in the range of 0 – 5V.

The working of this project of Arduino based digital DC voltmeter is explained here. In the digital voltmeter, the voltage that is measured in the analog form will be converted to its corresponding digital value using an Analog to Digital Converter.

 

In the first circuit

whose measurement range is from 0 to 5V, the input will be taken on Analog pin0. The analog pin will read any value from 0 to 1024. Then this analog value will be converted to digital by multiplying it by total voltage, which is 5V and dividing it by total resolution, which is 1024.

key differences between Arduino Uno and ESP32 in the context of our project

Arduino Uno

 

Microcontroller

 

Arduino Uno is based on the ATmega328P microcontroller.

 

Processing Power

The ATmega328P on Arduino Uno has lower processing power compared to ESP32.

 

Connectivity

Arduino Uno has limited connectivity options, typically relying on USB for serial communication.

 

Wireless Capabilities

Arduino Uno lacks built-in wireless capabilities. Adding WiFi or Bluetooth functionality may require external modules.

 

Power Consumption

Arduino Uno generally has lower power consumption compared to the ESP32.

 

I/O Pins

Arduino Uno provides a moderate number of digital and analog I/O pins.

 

ESP32 WiFi Module

 

Microcontroller:

ESP32 integrates a more powerful dual-core Xtensa LX6 microcontroller.

 

Processing Power

ESP32 offers significantly higher processing power compared to Arduino Uno.

 

Connectivity

ESP32 comes with built-in WiFi and Bluetooth capabilities, making it suitable for wireless communication.

 

Wireless Capabilities

ESP32 has native support for WiFi and Bluetooth, streamlining wireless communication without the need for additional modules.

 

Power Consumption

Depending on usage, ESP32 may have higher power consumption, especially when utilizing WiFi features.

 

I/O Pins

ESP32 provides a larger number of digital and analog I/O pins compared to Arduino Uno.

 

 

Considerations for our Energy Consumption Management System

 

Sensor Integration

As our system involves basic sensor interfacing and low-power requirements, Arduino Uno may suffice.

 

Wireless Communication

As of our plan to send data to the cloud wirelessly and require built-in WiFi capabilities, the ESP32 might be a better choice.

Processing Requirements

Consider the complexity of data processing and visualization. As our project involves complex computations or graphics, the ESP32's higher processing power may be beneficial.

 

Power Consumption

As of power efficiency is a critical factor, Arduino Uno's generally lower power consumption may be advantageous.
