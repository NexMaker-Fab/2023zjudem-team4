We have tried two assemble models here with two approaches to the assemble printing.

 

Model 1 - Lego Robot
This model is an example of when you design and print each part separately, and then join them together using glue, screws, or other fasteners. This method allows you to print larger objects that do not fit in the print volume of your printer, or to use different colors and materials for different parts. However, this method also requires more post-processing, such as sanding, cleaning, and aligning the parts. You also need to consider the tolerances and clearances of the parts, and how they will fit together.

Model 2 - Assembled Fish
This model is when you design and print the parts in such a way that they can be assembled without any additional fasteners. This method uses features such as snap-fits, hinges, dovetails, or interlocking joints to connect the parts. This method reduces the need for post-processing, and can create more seamless and functional assemblies. However, this method also requires more careful design, and may not work for all types of objects. You also need to consider the print orientation, layer direction, and support material of the parts, and how they will affect the assembly.
